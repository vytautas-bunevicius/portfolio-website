Podcast Reviews: What's the Buzz?
I dove into a massive dataset of 2 million podcast reviews across 100,000 shows to see what's clicking with listeners. Stored in a SQLite database with tables for categories, podcasts, reviews, and runs, it's a goldmine of listener vibes. The goal? Figure out what makes a podcast pop, how ratings shake out, and what's behind the chatter.
Project Approach
Data Prep: Pulled the data with SQLite and Pandas, scrubbed it clean—dropped duplicates, fixed dates, and added a review length column.

Exploration: Asked big questions: Which shows get the most reviews? Do long reviews mean high ratings? What's hot by category or over time? Used charts to spot trends.

Stats Check: Tested hypotheses—like whether review length ties to ratings—with tools like ANOVA.

Sentiment Peek: Dug into review text to see what words pop up in raves vs. rants.

Results: Found top podcasts, rating patterns, and even seasonal quirks—all laid out in a Looker dashboard.
Key Findings:

- Data Setup: Started with 2 million reviews, axed 655 duplicates. Cleaned text and added review lengths. Four tables: categories (212k rows), podcasts (110k), reviews (2M), and runs (16).

- Popularity: "Crime Junkie" crushed it with the most reviews. Interestingly, no correlation between review count and average rating (-0.08).

- Review Length: ANOVA showed significant relationship between review length and ratings (p ≈ 0, F = 232)—higher ratings often come with more words.

- Ratings Distribution: 86.7% of reviews are 5 stars. Low scores (1-4) are rare, under 6% each.

- Categories: "Society-Culture" leads with 16k+ podcasts and abundant 5-star reviews. "TV-Film" and "Sports" show more rating variety.

- Time Trends: Reviews peaked in June 2020, ratings peaked in 2018. January sees most reviews (164k), December least (136k). February has highest ratings (4.66), December lowest (4.61).

- Top Reviewers: Most active reviewer contributed 612 reviews. Four of top five reviewers consistently give 5 stars, while one averages 4.04.
Future Directions:

- Conduct surveys with top reviewers to understand their motivation
- Implement advanced NLP for theme extraction and success prediction
- Develop predictive models for ratings and trends

This analysis provides valuable insights for podcast creators and platforms, revealing what resonates with listeners and how engagement patterns evolve over time.
